# Convia Devpost Cover Design

## Goal

Create one 3:2 raster cover for the Convia OpenAI Build Week submission. It must remain legible as a small Devpost thumbnail and immediately communicate that Convia is a messenger where AI participates in a conversation between real people.

## Approved Direction

Use a polished dark, ChatGPT-like messaging environment rather than an abstract technology graphic. Show Judy and Haland as two distinct human participants and Convia as a third, clearly independent AI presence inside the same conversation. Convia should feel like a calm luminous participant, not a bot impersonating either person.

## Composition

- Canvas ratio: exactly 3:2, landscape.
- Central focus: a clean three-party conversation arranged around a restrained chat interface.
- Title: `Convia`, large and readable at thumbnail size.
- Supporting idea: subtle visual connection among Judy, Haland, and Convia without adding explanatory small text.
- Keep important content inside a generous safe area so Devpost cropping does not remove it.

## Visual Language

- Dark graphite background with soft green, teal, and warm-white light.
- Premium editorial product illustration with restrained depth and subtle glass-like interface layers.
- Human, calm, and relationship-oriented rather than cyberpunk or corporate.
- Strong contrast and a simple silhouette hierarchy suitable for a hackathon gallery.

## Constraints

- No OpenAI, ChatGPT, Devpost, YouTube, or Notion logos.
- No copied third-party interface, trademarked icon, watermark, tiny text, code, or fake metrics.
- No extra people or unrelated devices.
- Preserve the product thesis: AI has its own identity and participates between people.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The delivered PNG is exactly 3:2 and no larger than Devpost's 5 MB limit.
- `Convia` is spelled correctly and is the only required visible text.
- Judy, Haland, and Convia are visually distinguishable.
- The image reads clearly at thumbnail size and does not imply that Convia impersonates either user.
